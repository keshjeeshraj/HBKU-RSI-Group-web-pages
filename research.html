Mechanical & Energy Systems

In last few decades, ozone layer depletion, climate change and global warming emerged as one of the biggest threat to the future of mankind. The effects of these active phenomena and all other associated facts has been witnessed repeatedly by the mankind in the form of increased frequency of floods and storms, bad air quality, food and water insecurity, disruption of natural cycles in terms of times and flow of different components, etc. at various locations in the world. Moreover, scientists all over the world consider 1) usage of fossil fuels for energy needs, and 2) inefficient utilization of generated energy as the major reasons of these phenomena. For this reason, there is a strong global consensus to replace fossil fuels with renewable energy and give considerable attention to the efficiency of the energy systems.

Our goal is to develop such sustainable and resilient multigeneration energy systems which address both of the issues mentioned above – we focus on generation of energy in an environmentally benign way and its utilization in the most efficient way possible by minimizing the wastage of energy. In addition, to ensure continuous and stable operations in various changing conditions, we try to make the systems as much resilient and stable as possible. These goals are achieved by developing integrated energy systems having renewable energy sources (solar, biomass, wind, ocean, geothermal, etc.) as inputs, which produces several outputs needed (electricity, fresh water, domestic water heating, HVAC, Hydrogen, Oxygen, etc.) by the targeted sector. Resilience is established in the system by incorporation of various energy storage media and alternate energy generation methods (e.g. integration of H2-O2 combustor in the system). Furthermore, some of the sectors which we are working in include agriculture, transportation, tourism, buildings and water treatment.

Decision Sciences & Sustainability Analytics

Under the theme of decision sciences and sustainability analytics, the three research programs that are currently underway includes: (a) sustainable supply chains; (b) remote sensing and GIS; and (c)sustainability analytics.

Sustainable supply chains is the management of material, information and capital flows as well as cooperation among companies along the supply chain while integrating goals from all three dimensions of sustainable development, i.e., economic, environmental and social, which are derived from customer and stakeholder requirements. The research topics under this program includes: (a) modelling LNG supply chains; and (b) circular economies (industrial symbiosis).

Spatial data collected from satellites provides a broader areal coverage for analysis as opposed to single point measurements. This information can then be processed/modelled using machine learning and represented through geographic information systems (GIS), aiding policy and decision makers to undertake informed decisions. The research topics under this program includes the application of satellite monitoring for: (a) agricultural purposes, e.g. to monitor soil and crop conditions, assess soil mositure and irrigation requirements, better forecast of precipitation, early detection of pest infections, and quantification of crop yield; and (b) modelling the urban heat island (UHI) effect together with the time series data of weather related parameters such as air temperature and humidity.

In addition to the implementation of predictive analytics and learning algorithms to model and assess the significance of relationships between various environmental parameters, it has been recognised that there exists a niche opportunity to bring together the research conducted within the group on an integrated and interactive platform that effectively links models, provides visualisations, and thereby enable a holistic decision framework. The research topics under this program includes: (a) building a decision theatre for food security; and (b) implementing the blockchain technology for use cases peratining to supply chains, food security and circular economy.
